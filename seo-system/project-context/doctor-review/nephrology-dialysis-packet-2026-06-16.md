## B1. The 5 Stages of Chronic Kidney Disease
Graded by eGFR (a measure of kidney filtering):
- **Stage 1 (eGFR 90+):** normal function but signs of damage; control diabetes/BP.
- **Stage 2 (eGFR 60–89):** mild loss; usually symptom-free.
- **Stage 3a/3b (eGFR 45–59 / 30–44):** moderate loss; most patients first referred to a nephrologist here.
- **Stage 4 (eGFR 15–29):** severe loss; begin preparing for dialysis access or transplant.
- **Stage 5 (eGFR below 15):** kidney failure; dialysis or transplant needed.

**❓ Confirm:** eGFR ranges + "most referrals begin at Stage 3."

## B2. Hemodialysis vs Peritoneal Dialysis
- **Hemodialysis (HD):** blood filtered by machine, ~3 sessions/week, ~4 hrs each, at our NephroPlus unit.
- **Peritoneal Dialysis (PD/CAPD):** uses the abdomen's lining; can be done at home, including overnight (automated PD).

**❓ Confirm:** gated on Section A. If HD-only, this whole sub-section is cut.

## B3. Dialysis or Kidney Transplant — patient options
Dialysis starts immediately, no surgery/donor needed. Transplant offers a fuller lifestyle and freedom from regular sessions, but means major surgery + lifelong immunosuppressant medication. Choice depends on age, health, donor availability, preference.

**❓ Confirm:** accurate, balanced summary.

## B4. Two new FAQs
- **"What is a normal creatinine and eGFR level?"** — normal eGFR is 90+; below 60 for 3+ months = CKD; below 15 = kidney failure.
- **"Can chronic kidney disease be reversed?"** — mostly not reversible, but early-stage progression can be slowed/stabilised; acute kidney injury is often reversible.

**❓ Confirm:** both answers, esp. "below 60 for 3 months = CKD" and "AKI often reversible."
