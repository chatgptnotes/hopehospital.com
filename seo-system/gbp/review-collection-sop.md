## The 3-step routine — every satisfied patient, every day

**STEP 1 — ASK (at discharge / end of a good OPD visit)**
Front desk / nurse / coordinator says:
> *"I'm glad we could help you. If you were happy with your care, would you take 1 minute to leave us a Google review? It really helps other families. Here's the QR code — just scan and tap the stars."*

**STEP 2 — CHECK FIRST if they were unhappy** ⚠️ *(most important rule)*
If the patient seems upset or complains, **do NOT ask for a Google review.**
Instead say: *"I'm sorry it wasn't a great experience — let me note your feedback and have someone call you."*
→ Take their number, log it, escalate to the manager. **Resolve privately. Never push an unhappy patient to Google.**

**STEP 3 — FOLLOW UP within 24 hours (WhatsApp from 9823555053)**

> Namaste 🙏 Thank you for choosing Hope Hospital, Nagpur. If you had a good experience, a quick
> Google review helps other families find us: https://g.page/r/CXNeWp4Fw2cOEBM/review

| Role | Action |
|---|---|
| **Front desk / discharge** | Ask + show QR at discharge & billing |
| **Nurses / OPD coordinators** | Ask happy OPD & ward patients; show QR card |
| **Reception** | Send the 24-hour WhatsApp follow-up daily |
| **Manager** | Reply to every Google review within 48h; handle escalated complaints |

## Rules — do & don't
- ✅ Ask **only** genuinely satisfied patients. Make it easy (QR + tap).
- ✅ Reply to **every** review (positive and negative) within 48 hours.
- ❌ **Never** offer money, discounts, or gifts for reviews (against Google rules).
- ❌ **Never** post fake reviews or ask staff/family to post.
- ❌ **Never** ask all patients on the same day in bulk — keep it steady and natural.
- ❌ **Never** send an unhappy patient to Google — resolve it offline first.
