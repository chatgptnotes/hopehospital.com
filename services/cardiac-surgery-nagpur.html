Cardiac Care at Hope Hospital Nagpur

Hope Hospital Nagpur's Cardiology Department is equipped to handle the full spectrum of heart conditions — from routine cardiac evaluations and preventive cardiology to complex interventional procedures and emergency cardiac care. Our dedicated team of cardiologists, cardiac surgeons, and intensivists work together to deliver comprehensive, round-the-clock heart care.

Hope Hospital is empaneled under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY), MJPJAY, CGHS, and ECHS — making advanced heart surgery and cardiac interventions accessible and affordable for all patients in Nagpur and Central India.

warning Heart Attack Warning Signs — Act Immediately

  • Crushing chest pain, tightness, or pressure — may radiate to left arm, jaw, or back
  • Sudden shortness of breath at rest or with minimal activity
  • Cold sweats, nausea, or vomiting with no obvious cause
  • Sudden dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat (palpitations)
  • Unexplained severe fatigue or anxiety

If you experience any of these symptoms, call 071-22980073 immediately. Do not wait or drive yourself.

Cardiac Procedures & Services at Hope Hospital

monitor_heart

Interventional Cardiology

Angiography (coronary catheterization) and angioplasty (PTCA with stent implantation) for blocked coronary arteries. Performed in a state-of-the-art cath lab with high-resolution fluoroscopic imaging for precise, minimally invasive treatment.

emergency

Heart Attack Emergency (STEMI Care)

24/7 rapid response for ST-elevation myocardial infarction (heart attack). Our cardiac team is trained in primary PCI (primary angioplasty), which is the gold standard for restoring blood flow within the critical "golden hour" window.

local_hospital

Cardiac ICU (CICU)

30-bed ICU with dedicated cardiac monitoring, ventilators, defibrillators, intra-aortic balloon pump (IABP), and continuous specialist supervision. Critical for post-cardiac surgery recovery and unstable angina management.

favorite

Echocardiography (Echo)

Comprehensive 2D and Doppler echocardiography for diagnosis of valve disorders, heart failure, cardiomyopathy, pericardial effusion, and congenital heart disease. Available as both diagnostic and stress echo (stress echo) testing.

electrical_services

Pacemaker Implantation

Temporary and permanent pacemaker implantation for bradyarrhythmias (slow heart rhythms) and heart block. Also includes ICD (implantable cardioverter-defibrillator) implantation for patients at risk of sudden cardiac death.

directions_run

Cardiac Diagnostics

Comprehensive cardiac workup including TMT (treadmill test / stress ECG), Holter monitoring, chest X-ray, ECG, and cardiac enzyme testing. Essential for risk stratification and diagnosis of coronary artery disease.

What are the symptoms of a heart attack? +

Chest pain or pressure (may radiate to left arm or jaw), shortness of breath, cold sweats, nausea, sudden dizziness, and severe fatigue. If you experience these, call 071-22980073 immediately. Every minute matters.

What is the difference between angiography and angioplasty? +

Angiography is a diagnostic procedure that uses dye and X-rays to visualize blockages in coronary arteries. Angioplasty (PTCA) is a therapeutic procedure where a balloon catheter is used to open the blocked artery, often followed by stent placement to keep it open. Angiography is usually performed first to identify blockages, followed by angioplasty if treatment is required.

Can heart disease be prevented? +

Yes. Up to 80% of heart disease is preventable through: regular exercise (30 min/day), healthy diet (low salt, low fat, high fiber), no smoking, controlling blood pressure, diabetes, and cholesterol, maintaining healthy weight, and managing stress. Annual cardiac checkups are recommended for anyone above 40 or with a family history of heart disease.
