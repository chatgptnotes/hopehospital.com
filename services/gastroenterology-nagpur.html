Gastroenterology in Nagpur — Overview

Digestive problems affect one in four adults in India, yet most patients delay treatment for months — managing symptoms with antacids, ignoring blood in the stool, or assuming persistent stomach pain will resolve on its own. At NABH-accredited Hope Hospital Nagpur, our specialist gastroenterology team provides accurate diagnosis and effective treatment for the full range of GI and liver conditions, from common acidity and irritable bowel syndrome to complex liver disease, ERCP for bile duct stones, and early colorectal cancer detection through colonoscopy.

Our dedicated endoscopy suite is equipped with Olympus high-definition video gastroscopes and colonoscopes with narrow-band imaging (NBI) — technology that reveals subtle mucosal changes invisible to standard scopes. All procedures are performed under conscious sedation by an experienced anesthesiologist, so patients feel no discomfort. Most endoscopy and colonoscopy patients are discharged the same day and resume normal activity the following morning.

Hope Hospital is fully empaneled under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY), MJPJAY, CGHS, ECHS, and major private insurers. Endoscopy, colonoscopy, ERCP, and liver procedures are covered for eligible patients.

GI Emergency — Bleeding, Jaundice & Severe Abdominal Pain

Vomiting blood, passing black tarry stools, sudden severe abdominal pain, or rapidly deepening jaundice are GI emergencies. Do not take antacids or wait for symptoms to improve — these can indicate a perforated ulcer, acute pancreatitis, ruptured oesophageal varices (liver failure complication), or acute cholangitis (infected bile duct). Call 071-22980073 immediately or come directly to Hope Hospital's 24/7 emergency. Every hour of delay worsens outcomes in GI emergencies.

Acidity, GERD & Stomach Ulcers

Chronic acidity (GERD) causing heartburn, regurgitation, or chest pain is diagnosed with upper GI endoscopy and treated with a structured course of proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), lifestyle modification, and — when medical therapy fails — laparoscopic anti-reflux surgery (Nissen fundoplication). Peptic ulcers caused by H. pylori infection are cured with a 14-day antibiotic eradication regimen confirmed by follow-up testing. Bleeding ulcers are treated endoscopically (injection, clipping, or thermal coagulation) without surgery in most cases.

water_drop

Liver Disease & Hepatitis

Comprehensive liver care covering: Hepatitis B — antiviral therapy (tenofovir/entecavir) with 6-monthly monitoring; Hepatitis C — 12-week curative treatment with direct-acting antivirals (DAAs) achieving 95%+ cure rates; fatty liver disease (NAFLD/NASH) — structured weight loss, metabolic control, and FibroScan monitoring for fibrosis progression; alcoholic liver disease; cirrhosis management with ascites drainage, diuretics, and variceal band ligation to prevent bleeding; and liver cancer screening with ultrasound and AFP.

bubble_chart

Pancreatitis & Pancreatic Disease

Acute pancreatitis (severe epigastric pain radiating to the back, often triggered by gallstones or alcohol) is managed with IV fluids, pain control, and nutrition support — and ERCP to remove gallstones from the bile duct when the cause is biliary. Chronic pancreatitis with recurrent pain and enzyme insufficiency is managed with pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT), pain management, and endoscopic drainage of obstructed pancreatic ducts. Pancreatic cysts and tumors are evaluated with MRI, endoscopic ultrasound (EUS), and biopsy.

Upper GI Endoscopy — What to Expect

Fast for 6–8 hours before arrival. A thin, flexible HD camera is passed through the mouth into the stomach and first part of the small bowel under conscious sedation — most patients feel nothing and have no memory of the procedure. Takes 10–15 minutes. Biopsy (tissue sample) is taken if any abnormality is found. You rest in recovery for 45–60 minutes, then are discharged. Results of any biopsy are available in 5–7 working days. No driving on the day of the procedure — arrange transport.

Colonoscopy & Cancer Screening

Colorectal cancer is the third most common cancer in India and is curable if caught early. Colonoscopy examines the entire large bowel under conscious sedation — takes 20–30 minutes. Any polyps found are removed immediately (polypectomy) during the same sitting, preventing them from becoming cancerous. Recommended for all adults above 45, for any episode of rectal bleeding, for chronic change in bowel habits, and for anyone with a family history of colorectal cancer. A clear bowel preparation the evening before is required.

ERCP — Bile Duct Stone Removal

ERCP (Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography) is the gold-standard treatment for gallstones that have migrated from the gallbladder into the bile duct, causing obstructive jaundice, cholangitis (infected bile duct — a medical emergency), or pancreatitis. Under sedation, a side-viewing scope reaches the bile duct opening (ampulla of Vater), the duct is cut open (sphincterotomy), and stones are extracted with a balloon or basket — without any external incision. Stents are placed for bile duct strictures or tumors causing blockage.

Warning Signs — Do Not Ignore These Digestive Symptoms

Many serious GI conditions present early with symptoms that patients dismiss as "normal stomach trouble." Seek immediate evaluation if you have any of the following:

warning Seek Urgent GI Evaluation For:

  • Blood in the stool (bright red) or black, tarry, foul-smelling stools — possible GI bleeding
  • Vomiting blood or material that looks like coffee grounds — upper GI bleeding emergency
  • Yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice) — possible liver, bile duct, or pancreatic problem
  • Unexplained weight loss of more than 5 kg over 3 months — possible GI malignancy
  • Difficulty swallowing food or liquids that is progressive (dysphagia)
  • Sudden severe upper abdominal pain radiating to the back — possible acute pancreatitis
  • Abdomen swelling with visible fluid (ascites) — possible liver failure or cancer
  • Persistent abdominal pain lasting more than 2 weeks without diagnosis

Call 071-22980073 for urgent gastroenterology evaluation — same-day appointments are available for urgent symptoms.

What is the treatment for fatty liver (NAFLD) in Nagpur? +

Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) is treated primarily through lifestyle modification — structured weight loss (5–10% of body weight significantly reduces liver fat), dietary changes (reducing refined carbohydrates, sugar, and saturated fat), regular aerobic exercise, and tight control of diabetes and triglycerides. In advanced cases (NASH with fibrosis), the gastroenterologist monitors liver fibrosis with FibroScan and manages metabolic risk factors aggressively. Regular 6-monthly follow-up with liver function tests and ultrasound is recommended. Alcohol must be completely avoided regardless of severity.

What symptoms need an immediate gastroenterology consultation? +

See a gastroenterologist urgently if you have: blood in the stool or black tarry stools (possible bleeding in the GI tract), yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice), unexplained weight loss of more than 5 kg over 3 months, difficulty swallowing food or liquids, persistent vomiting, sudden severe abdominal pain, or swelling of the abdomen with visible fluid (ascites). These symptoms require same-day or next-day evaluation and should not be managed with over-the-counter medications alone.

How is Hepatitis B treated at Hope Hospital Nagpur? +

Chronic Hepatitis B is managed with antiviral therapy — tenofovir or entecavir — which suppresses the virus, prevents progression to cirrhosis, and significantly reduces the risk of liver cancer. Not all patients require medication; the decision depends on viral load (HBV DNA), liver enzyme levels (ALT), and presence of fibrosis on FibroScan or liver biopsy. Patients on antiviral therapy require 6-monthly monitoring of liver function tests, HBV DNA, and periodic liver cancer screening with ultrasound and AFP. Hope Hospital's gastroenterologist provides complete Hepatitis B management from diagnosis through long-term monitoring.

What is the difference between IBS and IBD? +

IBS (Irritable Bowel Syndrome) is a functional disorder — the bowel is structurally normal but behaves abnormally, causing alternating constipation and diarrhea, bloating, and abdominal cramps without visible inflammation. IBD (Inflammatory Bowel Disease) — Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis — involves actual inflammation of the bowel wall seen on colonoscopy and biopsy, and can cause complications like strictures, fistulas, and colorectal cancer over time. IBS is managed with dietary changes and specific medications. IBD requires specialist care with immunosuppressive or biologic therapy and regular endoscopic monitoring. A colonoscopy will clarify the diagnosis if you are unsure.

How do I prepare for an endoscopy or colonoscopy at Hope Hospital? +

For upper GI endoscopy: fast for 6–8 hours before the procedure. Arrive 30 minutes early. Take essential morning medications with a small sip of water unless instructed otherwise. For colonoscopy: bowel preparation starts the evening before with a laxative solution prescribed by your doctor — complete bowel emptying is essential for a clear view. Fast from midnight before the procedure. Arrange for someone to drive you home as sedation will be used. Biopsy results are usually available within 5–7 working days.
