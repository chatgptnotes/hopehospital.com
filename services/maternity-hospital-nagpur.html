Maternity Care in Nagpur — Overview

Choosing a maternity hospital is one of the most important decisions an expecting family makes. At NABH-accredited Hope Hospital, Kamptee Road Nagpur, our obstetrics and gynecology team provides the full spectrum of care — from the first antenatal visit through delivery and postpartum recovery. An obstetrician, anesthesiologist, and neonatologist are available on-site 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, so that every delivery — planned or unexpected — has the right specialist present.

We support natural childbirth wherever it is safe. For mothers who need or prefer a caesarean section, our obstetric operation theatre is equipped for both elective and emergency procedures with immediate neonatal resuscitation capability. A Level II+ NICU with round-the-clock neonatologist coverage is directly adjacent to the labour room — so premature or unwell newborns transition to intensive care within seconds of birth.

Hope Hospital is empaneled under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY), MJPJAY, CGHS, ECHS, and all major private insurers. Normal delivery, caesarean section, and NICU care are covered for eligible patients — completely free of cost.

Obstetric Emergency — 24/7 at Hope Hospital Nagpur

Sudden severe abdominal pain, heavy vaginal bleeding, absent fetal movements, severe headache with swollen face, or labour pains before 37 weeks are obstetric emergencies. Do not wait — come directly to Hope Hospital's emergency entrance on Kamptee Road, Teka Naka, or call 0712-2980073 immediately. Our obstetric team is on call round the clock with an emergency OT that can be activated within minutes.

What maternity & gynecology services does Hope Hospital offer?

pregnant_woman

Normal (Vaginal) Delivery

We actively support natural childbirth for all low-risk and many moderate-risk pregnancies. Continuous electronic fetal monitoring (CTG) during labour tracks baby's heartbeat in real time. Our skilled midwifery and obstetric nursing team provides one-on-one labour support. Birth companion (one family member) is permitted in the labour room to provide emotional support throughout the process.

self_improvement

Painless Delivery (Epidural)

Epidural labour analgesia eliminates or greatly reduces labour pain while keeping the mother fully conscious and able to push. A fine catheter is placed in the lower back by our experienced anesthesiologist, delivering continuous local anaesthetic throughout active labour. Fetal heart rate monitoring continues uninterrupted. Epidural analgesia does not increase the chance of C-section and is safe for the baby.

medical_services

Caesarean Section (C-Section)

Planned (elective) C-sections are scheduled at 39 weeks for medical indications. Emergency C-sections are performed within 30 minutes of decision when the baby or mother is in danger — our dedicated obstetric OT ensures this response time. Spinal anaesthesia allows the mother to remain awake and see her baby immediately after birth. Scar management and wound care protocols minimize healing time and scar visibility.

monitor_heart

High-Risk Pregnancy Care

Specialized management for gestational diabetes (GDM) with diet counselling, insulin therapy, and glucose monitoring; pre-eclampsia with anti-hypertensive therapy and fetal surveillance; placenta previa and placental abruption with planned delivery timing; twin and triplet pregnancies with detailed growth monitoring; and pregnancies complicated by heart disease, kidney disease, or thyroid disorders managed in collaboration with relevant specialists on-site.

favorite

Gynecology & Women's Health

Complete gynecology care covering PCOD/PCOS management with hormonal therapy and ovulation induction; uterine fibroid removal (laparoscopic myomectomy and hysteroscopic myomectomy) without removing the uterus; endometriosis surgery; ovarian cyst removal; abnormal uterine bleeding workup with hysteroscopy; laparoscopic hysterectomy for benign uterine disease; and pelvic floor repair for prolapse.

child_care

Newborn Care & NICU

All newborns receive Apgar scoring, warmth, early breastfeeding support, BCG and Hepatitis B vaccination, and a complete examination by a neonatologist before discharge. Babies needing intensive care transfer immediately to our Level II+ NICU — equipped for mechanical ventilation, CPAP, phototherapy, IV nutrition, and continuous monitoring. Parents receive daily updates and are encouraged to participate in kangaroo mother care for stable premature babies.

What are the warning signs during pregnancy?

Certain symptoms during pregnancy require emergency evaluation without delay. Go to Hope Hospital or call 0712-2980073 immediately if you experience:

warning Do Not Wait — Seek Emergency Care For:

  • Severe headache, blurred vision, or seeing flashing lights (signs of pre-eclampsia)
  • Sudden swelling of face, hands, or feet, especially in the third trimester
  • Heavy vaginal bleeding at any stage of pregnancy
  • Reduced or absent fetal movements after 28 weeks
  • Regular contractions or abdominal tightening before 37 weeks (preterm labour)
  • Sudden severe abdominal pain — possible placental abruption
  • Leaking fluid from the vagina before labour begins (ruptured membranes)
  • Fever above 38°C with chills during pregnancy

Call 0712-2980073 — Hope Hospital's obstetric emergency team is available 24/7.

Frequently Asked Questions — Maternity Hospital Nagpur

What is the cost of normal delivery in Nagpur at Hope Hospital? +

Normal (vaginal) delivery at Hope Hospital Nagpur costs approximately ₹20,000 to ₹40,000, depending on the duration of labour, anaesthesia used, and the number of antenatal monitoring sessions. This includes delivery charges, nursing care, and a standard 2-night stay in the maternity ward. Patients eligible under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY) or MJPJAY pay nothing — delivery is fully covered. Call 0712-2980073 for a package-wise breakdown.

Is C-section (caesarean delivery) covered under Ayushman Bharat in Nagpur? +

Yes. Both planned (elective) and emergency caesarean sections are covered under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY) and MJPJAY at Hope Hospital. Eligible patients pay zero out of pocket. CGHS and ECHS beneficiaries are also covered. Bring your Ayushman Card or Golden Card at the time of admission. For planned C-sections, we recommend pre-admission verification — call 0712-2980073 at least 1 week before your expected date.

What is painless delivery and is it available at Hope Hospital Nagpur? +

Painless delivery uses epidural analgesia — a thin catheter placed in the lower back that delivers local anaesthetic to block labour pain while keeping the mother fully awake and able to push. It is safe for both mother and baby when administered by an experienced anesthesiologist and monitored with continuous fetal heart rate tracking. Hope Hospital offers epidural labour analgesia during vaginal delivery. The additional cost for epidural analgesia is approximately ₹8,000–₹15,000 over the standard delivery charge. Call 0712-2980073 to plan your birth with epidural.

How do I know if I have a high-risk pregnancy? +

A pregnancy is considered high-risk if any of the following are present: age below 18 or above 35 years, pre-existing conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, kidney disease, or thyroid disorders; pregnancy complications like pre-eclampsia, placenta previa, gestational diabetes, Rh-negative blood group, multiple pregnancy (twins/triplets), or a history of previous pregnancy losses. If any of these apply, consult an obstetrician as early as possible — call 0712-2980073 to book a high-risk pregnancy consultation at Hope Hospital.

What is the NICU at Hope Hospital Nagpur equipped for? +

Hope Hospital's NICU can manage premature babies from 28 weeks gestation, very low birth-weight infants (under 1.5 kg), babies with respiratory distress requiring ventilatory support, neonates with birth asphyxia, neonatal jaundice requiring phototherapy, and newborns with sepsis or congenital abnormalities requiring stabilization. The unit is staffed with neonatologists 24/7 and equipped with mechanical ventilators, CPAP machines, radiant warmers, infusion pumps, and pulse oximeters for continuous monitoring.

What gynecology problems are treated at Hope Hospital Nagpur? +

Hope Hospital's gynecology team treats PCOD/PCOS with hormonal management and ovulation induction; uterine fibroids with laparoscopic or hysteroscopic myomectomy; endometriosis with laparoscopic excision; ovarian cysts — both laparoscopic removal and surveillance; abnormal uterine bleeding with hysteroscopy and endometrial ablation; prolapse surgery; and laparoscopic hysterectomy for benign uterine conditions. Fertility workup and basic infertility evaluation is also available.

Can uterine fibroids be removed without removing the uterus? +

Yes. For women who wish to preserve their uterus (especially those planning future pregnancies), laparoscopic myomectomy is the procedure of choice — fibroids are removed through small keyhole incisions while keeping the uterus intact. Hysteroscopic myomectomy is used for fibroids inside the uterine cavity and requires no external incisions. Hysterectomy is recommended only when fibroids are large, multiple, and symptomatic, and the patient does not wish to retain fertility. Your gynecologist will advise the best option based on your specific case.

What vaccinations does my newborn need after birth at Hope Hospital? +

At Hope Hospital, newborns receive BCG (tuberculosis), Hepatitis B (first dose), and OPV-0 (oral polio vaccine) within 24 hours of birth. Our pediatric team provides a complete immunization schedule card (per the Indian Academy of Pediatrics schedule) covering all vaccines due at 6 weeks, 10 weeks, 14 weeks, 6 months, 9 months, 12 months, and 15–18 months. The schedule card is given before discharge, with reminders for each due date.
