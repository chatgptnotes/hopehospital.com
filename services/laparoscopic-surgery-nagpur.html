Laparoscopic Surgery in Nagpur — Overview

Laparoscopic (keyhole) surgery has replaced open surgery as the gold standard for a wide range of abdominal conditions — from gallstones and hernias to appendicitis and obesity. At NABH-accredited Hope Hospital Nagpur, our experienced surgical team performs over 500 laparoscopic procedures annually, using high-definition 4K laparoscopic systems that deliver precision impossible with open surgical techniques. Patients from Nagpur, Vidarbha, and across Central India benefit from the shorter recovery, less pain, and smaller scars that keyhole surgery provides.

The fundamental advantage of laparoscopic surgery is that a surgeon can operate inside the abdomen through 3–4 incisions of 5–12 mm — each smaller than a fingertip — rather than cutting a 10–20 cm opening. A miniature HD camera inserted through one port transmits magnified images to a monitor, giving the surgical team a closer, clearer view of anatomy than open surgery. Advanced energy devices (harmonic scalpel, LigaSure) seal blood vessels without sutures, reducing blood loss and post-operative pain significantly.

Hope Hospital is fully empaneled under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY), MJPJAY, CGHS, ECHS, and 20+ government and insurance schemes. Gallbladder removal, hernia repair, and appendectomy are covered under these schemes for eligible patients — completely free of cost.

Surgical Emergency — Acute Appendicitis & Strangulated Hernia

Acute appendicitis (sudden severe right lower abdominal pain with fever) and strangulated hernia (a hernia that cannot be pushed back and turns painful and hard) are surgical emergencies requiring immediate treatment. Delay increases the risk of perforation, peritonitis, and life-threatening complications. Hope Hospital has 24/7 emergency surgical cover with round-the-clock OT availability. If you or a family member has sudden severe abdominal pain — call 0712-2980073 immediately.

Laparoscopic Procedures We Offer

bubble_chart

Gallbladder Removal (Cholecystectomy)

Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is the definitive treatment for gallstones, acute cholecystitis, and symptomatic gallbladder polyps. Four small ports replace one large incision — patients are typically discharged within 24 hours, eating normally the next day. For acute cholecystitis, early laparoscopic removal within 72 hours of symptom onset is safer than delayed surgery and reduces hospital stay.

healing

Hernia Repair (Inguinal, Umbilical & Incisional)

Laparoscopic totally extraperitoneal (TEP) and transabdominal preperitoneal (TAPP) repair for inguinal hernias. Laparoscopic mesh repair for umbilical and incisional hernias. Mesh placement eliminates tension on the repair, reducing recurrence below 2%. Both sides of a bilateral inguinal hernia can be fixed through the same three ports — a major advantage over open surgery which requires two separate incisions.

emergency

Appendix Removal (Appendectomy)

Laparoscopic appendectomy for acute and perforated appendicitis. Three small ports replace the conventional 5–8 cm right lower quadrant incision. The approach allows the entire abdomen to be inspected for other causes of pain — an advantage over open surgery. Hospital stay is typically 1–2 days for uncomplicated appendicitis, with return to normal activity in 1–2 weeks.

monitor_weight

Bariatric (Weight Loss) Surgery

Laparoscopic sleeve gastrectomy (removal of 80% of the stomach) and Roux-en-Y gastric bypass for patients with BMI above 32.5 with obesity-related conditions, or above 37.5 without. Both procedures achieve 60–80% excess weight loss within 18 months and dramatically improve or resolve type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and sleep apnoea. All bariatric candidates undergo pre-operative dietary, psychological, and cardiac assessment.

swap_vert

Anti-Reflux Surgery (GERD)

Laparoscopic Nissen fundoplication for gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) that has failed medical management or requires lifelong high-dose proton pump inhibitors. The fundus of the stomach is wrapped around the lower oesophagus to recreate the natural anti-reflux barrier. Over 90% of patients are free of reflux symptoms after surgery, with most discharged on day 2 and eating soft food within a week.

search

Diagnostic Laparoscopy

For unexplained chronic or recurrent abdominal pain where CT, MRI, and endoscopy have not identified a cause. A single laparoscopic port allows direct visualization of all abdominal organs — liver, gallbladder, stomach, small bowel, colon, appendix, ovaries, and uterus. Biopsies, adhesion release, and drainage of loculated collections can be performed during the same procedure, making diagnostic laparoscopy both diagnostic and potentially therapeutic.

Warning Signs — When to See a Laparoscopic Surgeon

Several abdominal conditions worsen rapidly if untreated. Seek urgent evaluation if you have any of the following:

warning Do Not Ignore These Symptoms:

  • Recurring pain in the upper right abdomen, especially after fatty or fried meals (gallstones)
  • Sudden severe abdominal pain starting around the navel and shifting to the right lower abdomen (appendicitis)
  • Fever with abdominal pain and vomiting — may indicate acute cholecystitis or appendicitis
  • Yellowish skin or eyes with abdominal pain (jaundice — blocked bile duct from gallstones)
  • A bulge in the groin or around the navel that appears on standing or straining
  • A hernia that is painful, hard, and cannot be pushed back — go to emergency immediately (strangulation)
  • Severe heartburn or regurgitation not controlled by medication for more than 3 months

Is laparoscopic hernia surgery better than open surgery? +

For most hernia types, laparoscopic (keyhole) repair offers significant advantages over open surgery: smaller incisions (3 ports of 5–12 mm instead of one large cut), less post-operative pain, lower risk of wound infection, faster return to work (1–2 weeks vs 4–6 weeks), and lower recurrence rates when mesh is used correctly. Laparoscopic repair is particularly preferred for bilateral inguinal hernias and recurrent hernias. Your surgeon will advise whether laparoscopic or open repair is more appropriate for your specific hernia.

How long is the hospital stay after laparoscopic surgery? +

Most laparoscopic procedures at Hope Hospital allow same-day or next-day discharge. Laparoscopic cholecystectomy (gallbladder removal) typically requires a 24-hour stay. Appendectomy for uncomplicated appendicitis requires 1–2 days. Laparoscopic hernia repair is often done as a day-care procedure with discharge the same evening. More complex procedures like bariatric surgery require 2–3 days. Patients are usually walking within hours of surgery, and most can return to desk work within 5–7 days.

Is gallbladder surgery covered under Ayushman Bharat in Nagpur? +

Yes. Hope Hospital is empaneled under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY) and MJPJAY. Laparoscopic cholecystectomy (gallbladder removal) is a covered procedure under both schemes. Eligible patients pay nothing out of pocket — bring your Ayushman Card or Golden Card to the hospital. CGHS and ECHS beneficiaries are also covered. Call 0712-2980073 to confirm your eligibility before admission.

What are the signs that I need gallbladder surgery? +

You may need gallbladder surgery if you experience: recurring pain in the upper right side of your abdomen (especially after fatty meals), pain that radiates to the right shoulder or back, nausea and vomiting with abdominal pain, fever with chills and jaundice (indicating a blocked bile duct), or if an ultrasound has confirmed gallstones. Untreated gallstones can lead to acute cholecystitis, cholangitis, or pancreatitis — all of which require emergency surgery. An ultrasound abdomen and surgical consultation will confirm whether surgery is needed.

Can appendix surgery be done laparoscopically in Nagpur? +

Yes. Laparoscopic appendectomy is the preferred approach for appendicitis at Hope Hospital. It involves 3 small port incisions (5–10 mm) instead of a 5–8 cm open cut, resulting in less pain, lower infection risk, and faster recovery. Most patients are discharged within 24–48 hours. In cases of perforated appendicitis with peritonitis, laparoscopic surgery is still performed where feasible. Call 0712-2980073 immediately if you have sudden right lower abdominal pain with fever — appendicitis is a surgical emergency.

What is the recovery time after laparoscopic hernia repair? +

After laparoscopic hernia repair with mesh, most patients can walk the same evening and return to desk work within 1–2 weeks. Driving is safe after 3–5 days. Heavy lifting and strenuous activity should be avoided for 4–6 weeks to allow the mesh to integrate securely. The risk of hernia recurrence after laparoscopic mesh repair is less than 2% when performed by an experienced surgeon — significantly lower than open repair without mesh.

Is bariatric (weight loss) surgery available in Nagpur at Hope Hospital? +

Yes. Hope Hospital offers laparoscopic bariatric surgery for patients with a BMI above 32.5 with obesity-related conditions (diabetes, hypertension, sleep apnoea) or BMI above 37.5 without comorbidities. Procedures include laparoscopic sleeve gastrectomy (most common), Roux-en-Y gastric bypass, and revision bariatric surgery. Pre-operative workup includes dietary assessment, psychological evaluation, and cardiac clearance. Call 0712-2980073 for a bariatric surgery consultation.
