Hip Replacement Surgery in Nagpur — Overview

Hip replacement surgery (hip arthroplasty) is a procedure where a damaged or diseased hip joint is replaced with an artificial implant to eliminate pain and restore mobility. It is one of the most successful operations in modern orthopedics, with outcomes that dramatically improve quality of life for patients with severe hip arthritis, avascular necrosis (AVN), hip fractures, and rheumatoid arthritis.

At Hope Hospital Nagpur, hip replacements are performed by Dr. B.K. Murali using the latest implant systems and surgical techniques — including posterior, lateral, and minimally invasive anterior approaches — tailored to each patient's anatomy, age, and activity goals.

Hope Hospital is empaneled under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY), MJPJAY, CGHS, ECHS, and 20+ government health schemes. Eligible patients receive hip replacement surgery completely free of cost.

Types of Hip Replacement We Offer

accessibility_new

Total Hip Replacement (THR)

Both the acetabulum (socket) and femoral head (ball) are replaced with prosthetic components. Best for advanced hip osteoarthritis and AVN in younger, active patients. Implants last 20–30 years. Available in cemented, cementless, and hybrid fixation options.

radio_button_checked

Bipolar Hemiarthroplasty

Only the femoral head is replaced with a two-component (bipolar) prosthesis, preserving the natural acetabulum. Most suited for elderly patients with femoral neck fractures. Shorter surgery time and faster recovery compared to total hip replacement.

refresh

Revision Hip Replacement

Replacement of a previously implanted hip prosthesis that has loosened, worn out, or become infected. A complex procedure requiring specialized implants, bone grafting, and extensive surgical expertise. Dr. B.K. Murali performs both primary and revision hip surgeries.

water_drop

AVN Hip Replacement

Avascular necrosis (AVN) of the femoral head causes the bone to die due to disrupted blood supply. In Stage 3 and 4 AVN, total hip replacement is the definitive treatment. Hope Hospital has extensive experience in AVN hip replacement with excellent long-term outcomes.

Signs You May Need Hip Replacement Surgery

Consider consulting Dr. B.K. Murali if you experience any of the following:

  • Persistent hip or groin pain that limits walking, climbing stairs, or daily activities
  • Hip pain that continues even at rest or while sleeping
  • Stiffness in the hip joint that restricts movement or putting on shoes/socks
  • Limping due to hip pain or weakness
  • Failure of non-surgical treatments — physiotherapy, pain medications, injections — after 6+ months
  • X-ray showing advanced arthritis, AVN, or joint space narrowing
  • Hip fracture (femoral neck fracture) in elderly patients

How long does recovery take after hip replacement? +

Patients walk with a walker within 24–48 hours. Hospital stay is 4–5 days. Physiotherapy begins on day one. Return to daily activities in 6–8 weeks. Full recovery in 3–6 months.

What conditions require hip replacement surgery? +

Severe hip osteoarthritis, avascular necrosis (AVN), rheumatoid arthritis of the hip, femoral neck fractures in elderly patients, and post-traumatic arthritis — when non-surgical treatments have failed after 6+ months.

What is avascular necrosis (AVN) and does it need hip replacement? +

AVN is death of the femoral head bone due to disrupted blood supply — common with steroid use, alcohol, trauma, or sickle cell disease. In Stage 3 and 4 AVN, total hip replacement is the definitive treatment with excellent outcomes.

What is the difference between total hip replacement and bipolar hemiarthroplasty? +

THR replaces both the socket and ball — best for arthritis and AVN in active patients. Bipolar hemiarthroplasty replaces only the femoral head — best for elderly patients with femoral neck fractures needing a shorter, faster procedure.

How long does a hip replacement implant last? +

Modern hip implants last 20–30 years with proper care — maintaining healthy weight, following physiotherapy instructions, and attending regular follow-up appointments with Dr. Murali.
