What is Ayushman Bharat (PMJAY)?

Ayushman Bharat — Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PMJAY) is the world's largest government-funded health insurance scheme. Launched in 2018 by the Government of India, it provides cashless hospitalization up to Rs. 5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary medical care at empaneled hospitals across India.

Under PMJAY, 12.34 crore eligible families (about 55 crore people) receive free treatment for 1,949 medical procedures across 27 specialties — including all major surgeries like heart bypass, kidney transplant, cancer chemotherapy, knee replacement, brain surgery, and more.

Key Facts About Ayushman Bharat 2026

  • Cover amount: Rs. 5 lakh per family per year (no per-individual sub-limit)
  • Cashless: Zero out-of-pocket cost at empaneled hospitals
  • Procedures covered: 1,949 (all major surgeries + chemotherapy + dialysis + maternity)
  • Hospitals empaneled: 30,000+ across India (1,800+ in Maharashtra)
  • Recent expansion: All citizens above age 70 now automatically eligible (since Oct 2024) regardless of income
  • Pre-existing diseases: Covered from day 1
  • 3 days pre + 15 days post-hospitalization expenses covered

Who is Eligible for Ayushman Bharat in 2026?

Three main eligibility categories:

1. Families identified by SECC 2011

The Socio-Economic Caste Census 2011 identified families in deprivation categories. Most rural BPL families and urban occupation-based workers (street vendors, construction labour, domestic help, etc.) are automatically eligible.

2. Senior Citizens 70 years and above (New from Oct 2024)

All Indian citizens aged 70+ are now automatically covered under PMJAY, regardless of income or social category — known as the "Ayushman Vandana Yojana" expansion. They get a separate Rs. 5 lakh top-up.

3. State-Specific Scheme Beneficiaries

Several states converged their existing schemes with PMJAY. Maharashtra residents also benefit from MJPJAY (Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana), which extends coverage to additional families (yellow & orange ration card holders) and provides Rs. 1.5 lakh additional cover for renal diseases.

Documents Needed for Free Surgery

Bring these documents on the day of admission or OPD visit:

  1. Aadhaar card of the patient (mandatory for biometric verification)
  2. Ayushman Bharat card / e-card / PMJAY ID number (digital copy on phone is acceptable)
  3. Ration card or family ID (for family relationship verification)
  4. Doctor's prescription / referral recommending the surgery
  5. Previous medical records: reports, X-rays, scans, blood tests, discharge summaries
  6. Mobile number registered for PMJAY (for OTP verification)

Important — Pre-Authorization Required

For planned surgery, the hospital must submit a pre-authorization request to the State Health Authority (SHA) along with diagnosis, package code, estimated cost, and supporting reports. Approval typically takes 6-48 hours. For emergencies, treatment starts immediately and pre-auth is processed in parallel.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How many surgeries are covered under Ayushman Bharat PMJAY?

PMJAY covers 1,949 medical procedures across 27 specialties — cardiology, oncology, orthopedics, neurosurgery, nephrology, gastroenterology, OB-GYN, ENT, ophthalmology, plastic surgery, and pediatrics. Cover is up to Rs. 5 lakh per family per year, fully cashless.

Q: What surgeries are free under Ayushman Bharat at Hope Hospital Nagpur?

Free under PMJAY at Hope Hospital: knee/hip replacement, spine surgery, CABG, angioplasty, cancer surgery & chemotherapy, brain tumour surgery, kidney transplant, dialysis, laparoscopic gallbladder, hernia, hysterectomy, normal/C-section delivery, and 200+ procedures.

Q: What is the Ayushman Bharat package amount for major surgeries?

Sample PMJAY packages: Knee replacement Rs. 80,000–1,20,000 · Hip Rs. 90,000–1,40,000 · CABG Rs. 1,10,000–2,00,000 · Angioplasty + stent Rs. 60,000–1,00,000 · Kidney transplant Rs. 2,90,000–4,40,000 · Hysterectomy Rs. 25,000–50,000. Family cover: Rs. 5 lakh/year.

Q: Who is eligible for Ayushman Bharat in 2026?

Families identified under SECC 2011 (mostly BPL rural + occupation-based urban workers). From Oct 2024, all citizens aged 70+ are automatically covered regardless of income. Maharashtra residents also get MJPJAY. Verify at pmjay.gov.in or call 14555.

Q: What documents are needed for free surgery under Ayushman Bharat?

(1) Aadhaar card, (2) Ayushman Bharat card / e-card / PMJAY ID, (3) Ration card, (4) Doctor's prescription, (5) Previous medical records. At Hope Hospital, our insurance team verifies on the spot, free.

Q: Can I get free heart bypass surgery (CABG) under Ayushman Bharat in Nagpur?

Yes. CABG is fully covered under PMJAY (Rs. 1,10,000–2,00,000 package). Hope Hospital's Zion Cardiac Hospital performs cashless CABG, angioplasty, and emergency cardiac care for eligible patients.

Q: Is kidney transplant covered under Ayushman Bharat?

Yes. Kidney transplant is covered with a package of Rs. 2,90,000–4,40,000 covering donor surgery, recipient surgery, ICU, post-transplant medications. Hope Hospital (with NephroPlus partnership) is empaneled for cashless transplant.

Q: How long does Ayushman Bharat verification take at Hope Hospital?

On-the-spot, typically 15–30 minutes for eligibility verification. State Health Authority pre-authorization for surgery takes 6–48 hours. For emergencies, treatment starts immediately.

Q: Can I use Ayushman Bharat for chronic conditions like dialysis & chemotherapy?

Yes. Chronic procedures like hemodialysis (per session) and chemotherapy (per cycle) are covered under PMJAY. As long as the family's Rs. 5 lakh annual cover is available, sessions continue uninterrupted.

Q: Can I get treatment at Hope Hospital if I have an Ayushman card from another state?

Yes. Ayushman Bharat is a national portable scheme — you can avail cashless treatment at any empaneled hospital across India, including Hope Hospital Nagpur, regardless of which state issued your card.

Q: Are pre-existing diseases covered under Ayushman Bharat?

Yes. All pre-existing diseases are covered from day 1 of enrollment — there is no waiting period.

Q: What's the difference between PMJAY and MJPJAY?

PMJAY is the central scheme (Rs. 5 lakh family cover, nationwide). MJPJAY is Maharashtra state's scheme (additional cover for state residents). Most Maharashtra empaneled hospitals — including Hope Hospital — accept both. The two schemes are converged, so eligible patients get combined coverage.
