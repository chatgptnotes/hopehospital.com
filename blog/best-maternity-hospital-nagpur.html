Factors That Affect Delivery Cost in Nagpur

1. Type of Delivery — Normal vs C-Section

Normal vaginal delivery is the least expensive option, ranging from ₹20,000 to ₹40,000 at Hope Hospital. A C-section (caesarean) costs more due to the surgical team, operation theater, and longer recovery stay — typically ₹40,000 to ₹80,000 for private patients. Under Ayushman Bharat and MJPJAY, C-sections are covered completely free for eligible patients.

2. Painless Delivery (Epidural)

Epidural analgesia provides significant pain relief during labour. At Hope Hospital, an experienced anesthesiologist administers the epidural and monitors the mother throughout labour. The additional cost for epidural analgesia is approximately ₹8,000 to ₹15,000 over the base delivery charge. It does not increase the likelihood of a C-section when managed correctly.

3. Room Category

The type of hospital room chosen — general ward, semi-private, or private room — affects the overall cost. The medical component of delivery remains the same regardless of room type; room upgrades add ₹500 to ₹2,000 per day in room charges.

4. High-Risk Pregnancy Complications

Patients with gestational diabetes, pre-eclampsia, placenta previa, previous C-section, twin or triplet pregnancies, or medical conditions such as heart disease or renal disease may require additional monitoring, longer ICU stays, specialist consultations, or neonatal support — all of which can add to the total cost. Hope Hospital's multidisciplinary team manages these cases in-house.

5. NICU Requirement for the Newborn

If the newborn requires NICU admission — due to prematurity, low birth weight, jaundice, breathing difficulty, or infection — NICU charges are additional for private patients. Government scheme patients have NICU care covered under the scheme package.

NICU at Hope Hospital — Care for Your Newborn

Hope Hospital's on-site Level III NICU is equipped to handle the most critical newborn cases in Central India. The unit operates 24/7 and provides:

  • Premature baby care from 28 weeks gestation
  • Very low birth-weight (VLBW) infant management
  • Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS) treatment — including surfactant therapy and ventilator support
  • Phototherapy for neonatal jaundice
  • Neonatal sepsis management
  • Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) for stable preterm infants
  • Neonatology-trained nursing staff present 24/7

Having the NICU on-site means that if your baby needs intensive care immediately after birth, there is no delay or transfer to another facility. This is a critical safety advantage for high-risk deliveries.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cost of normal delivery in Nagpur?

Normal delivery cost in Nagpur at Hope Hospital ranges from ₹20,000 to ₹40,000 for private patients, depending on room type and duration of stay. Patients eligible under Ayushman Bharat, MJPJAY, CGHS, ECHS, or other government schemes can receive delivery care at zero cost. Call 071-22980073 to confirm your eligibility.

Is C-section free under Ayushman Bharat in Nagpur?

Yes. Hope Hospital is empaneled under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY) and MJPJAY. Eligible patients receive the complete C-section package — surgery, anaesthesia, hospital stay, and newborn care — at zero cost. Bring your Ayushman card or ration card to the hospital for same-day eligibility verification.

Is painless delivery (epidural) available in Nagpur?

Yes. Hope Hospital offers epidural analgesia for painless delivery. The epidural is placed by an experienced anesthesiologist so you remain awake and comfortable throughout labour. The additional cost is approximately ₹8,000 to ₹15,000 over normal delivery charges. Discuss this option during your antenatal visit to plan ahead.

Does Hope Hospital manage high-risk pregnancies?

Yes. Hope Hospital has a dedicated high-risk pregnancy team managing conditions such as gestational diabetes, pre-eclampsia, placenta previa, twin pregnancies, previous C-section, and pregnancies complicated by cardiac or renal disease. The on-site NICU and ICU ensure immediate support for both mother and baby.

What is the NICU facility at Hope Hospital?

Hope Hospital has an on-site Level III NICU that can care for premature babies from 28 weeks gestation, very low birth-weight infants, and newborns with Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS). The NICU is staffed 24/7 with neonatology-trained nurses and a paediatrician on call, so there is no need to transfer your baby to another facility.

Does Hope Hospital offer gynecology services beyond maternity?

Yes. The gynecology department treats PCOD/PCOS, uterine fibroids (laparoscopic myomectomy), endometriosis, ovarian cysts, abnormal uterine bleeding, and provides fertility consultations. Most procedures are available as minimally invasive laparoscopic surgeries for faster recovery and shorter hospital stays.
