What is Knee Replacement Surgery?

Knee replacement surgery, also called total knee arthroplasty (TKA) or total knee replacement (TKR), is a surgical procedure to replace damaged or diseased parts of your knee joint with artificial components made from metal alloys, high-grade plastics, and polymers.

The surgery involves removing damaged cartilage and bone from the surface of your knee joint and replacing them with prosthetic components that replicate the smooth surface of a healthy joint. This allows for pain-free movement and restoration of normal knee function.

When Do You Need Knee Replacement Surgery?

Dr. B.K. Murali, recognized as the best orthopedic surgeon in Nagpur, recommends knee replacement when conservative treatments have failed and knee pain significantly affects your quality of life. Here are the key indicators:

Common Symptoms That May Require Knee Replacement:

  • Severe knee pain that limits everyday activities like walking, climbing stairs, getting in and out of chairs, or even sleeping
  • Moderate to severe pain while resting, both day and night
  • Chronic knee inflammation and swelling that doesn't improve with rest or medications
  • Knee deformity - visible bowing in or out of your knee
  • Knee stiffness - inability to bend or straighten your knee fully
  • Failure of conservative treatments including medications, physical therapy, walking aids, or cortisone injections
  • Significant reduction in mobility affecting your independence and quality of life

Conditions That Lead to Knee Replacement:

  1. Osteoarthritis - The most common cause (90% of cases). Age-related "wear and tear" arthritis where cartilage gradually wears away
  2. Rheumatoid Arthritis - An autoimmune disease causing joint inflammation and damage
  3. Post-traumatic Arthritis - Arthritis following a severe knee injury or fracture
  4. Avascular Necrosis - Limited blood supply to the bone causing bone death
  5. Knee Deformities - Severe bowing of the knee requiring correction

Who is a Good Candidate for Knee Replacement?

There's no absolute age or weight restriction for this surgery. The decision is based on pain level, disability, and how it affects your life - not your age. Dr. Murali has successfully performed joint replacements on patients ranging from their 50s to their 80s at Hope Hospital.

Ideal candidates typically:

  • Have tried conservative treatments for at least 6 months without success
  • Experience pain that limits daily activities and affects quality of life
  • Are in overall good health to undergo surgery and rehabilitation
  • Have realistic expectations about surgery outcomes
  • Are committed to post-surgery rehabilitation

Pre-Surgery Preparation

  • Comprehensive Medical Evaluation - Blood tests, X-rays, ECG, and medical history review
  • Pre-anesthetic Checkup - Consultation with anesthesiologist to determine the best anesthesia option
  • Surgical Planning - Dr. Murali reviews your X-rays and plans the exact surgical approach
  • Pre-surgery Instructions - Fasting guidelines, medication adjustments, and hospital admission process

During Surgery (Step-by-Step)

  1. Anesthesia - Either spinal anesthesia (most common) or general anesthesia administered
  2. Incision - Dr. Murali makes a precise incision (typically 6-8 inches) over the knee
  3. Bone Preparation - Damaged cartilage and bone surfaces are carefully removed from the femur, tibia, and patella
  4. Implant Positioning - Metal and plastic components are precisely positioned and secured
  5. Testing - The knee is flexed and tested to ensure proper alignment and range of motion
  6. Closure - The incision is closed with sutures or staples, and a sterile dressing applied

Surgery Duration: Typically 1.5 to 2.5 hours

Hospital Stay: 3-5 days at Hope Hospital with 24/7 ICU monitoring

Frequently Asked Questions About Knee Replacement

Q: How long does a knee replacement last?

Modern knee implants typically last 15-20 years or more. With advances in implant technology and proper care, many patients never need revision surgery.

Q: Is knee replacement surgery painful?

You'll experience some pain after surgery, but it's well-managed with medications. Most patients report that post-surgery discomfort is far less than the chronic pain they experienced before surgery.

Q: Can I kneel or squat after knee replacement?

Most patients can kneel comfortably 3-6 months after surgery. Squatting ability varies by individual, but many patients can perform modified squats. Dr. Murali provides specific guidance based on your recovery.

Q: When can I return to work after knee replacement?

Most patients with desk jobs can return to work within 4-6 weeks. Those with physically demanding jobs may need 3-4 months. Dr. Murali will advise based on your specific occupation.

Q: Will I set off metal detectors at airports?

Yes, knee implants may trigger metal detectors. We provide you with a medical implant card to show security personnel.

Q: What activities can I do after knee replacement?

Low-impact activities like walking, swimming, golf, cycling, and dancing are encouraged. High-impact activities like running, jumping, or contact sports should be avoided to protect the implant.
